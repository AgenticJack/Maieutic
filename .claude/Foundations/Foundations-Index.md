The reports are compacted — citations (author/year), numbers, effect sizes, and named mechanisms are preserved; the narrative bulk is not. All reports live in `.claude/Foundations/`.

### AI-Second-Brain — *Vault Principles 1–10*
The vault's cognitive contract: retrieval before reveal; spaced surfacing of dormant notes; AI as interlocutor (not author) in generative states; generation before suggestion; metacognitive scaffolding via predict-then-observe; state-aware offloading; provenance preservation; germane-vs-extraneous friction budgets; restraint on uninvited synthesis; expertise-graded defaults. The "why" behind every Path B gate and the generative/completed distinction.

### ELM — *Learning Pipeline Principles 11–15*
The acquisition pipeline: problem-first sequencing (Prepare→Attempt→Consolidate; productive failure), Socratic-first interaction, generation-before-vault (generation effect), three-retrieval spaced scheduling with intervals from completion (+1/+6/+14), and objective calibration detection (the fluency illusion; why self-report is dropped; the ≥20-point calibration-gap signal). The empirical basis for the review schedule and the no-self-report rule.

### Narrative-Socratic — *Principle 16*
Narrative-Socratic teaching: the four mechanisms (causal chaining, situation models, emotional tagging, narrative transportation — Green & Brock 2000), why pure narrative and pure Socratic each fail alone, the hybrid architecture (narrate→question→resolve→deeper question), the author-character model (Klassen 2006 — narrated speech, never full inhabitation), the rediscovery/productive-confusion pattern, and the three studied teacher methods (Floathead Physics, Veritasium, 3Blue1Brown). **Read before every narrative session, alongside the Transcripts.**

### Cognitive-Development — *Principles 17–20*
The higher-order learning layer: interleaved session architecture at natural stopping points (Kornell & Bjork 2008; Rohrer et al. 2014; Taylor & Rohrer 2010); cross-domain analogical transfer / the Analogy Gate (Gick & Holyoak 1983; Gentner & Markman 1997; Novick 1988); dual-process metacognitive calibration and the U1–U5 scale (Bjork et al. 2013; Koriat & Bjork 2005; Evans & Stanovich 2013); schema externalization and mental-model audit (Chi et al. 1981; Novak & Cañas 2006; Schwartz & Bransford 1998).

### Discussion-Mode — *Principle 21*
Intellectual co-construction: exploratory vs cumulative vs disputational talk (Mercer 2000, 2007); ICAP — Interactive > Constructive > Active > Passive (Chi & Wylie 2014); authentic dissent over performed devil's advocate (Nemeth 1986); inquiry vs persuasion dialogue (Walton); dialogic teaching (Alexander 2008); Vygotsky (1978); argumentation development (Kuhn 1991, 2001). The basis for stating genuine positions, marking epistemic status, and the named/time-limited devil's-advocate role. **Read before every Discussion Mode session.**
